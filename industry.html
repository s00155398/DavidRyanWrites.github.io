Industry

Smoked oak, dark corridors and varnish

Fill the lungs of the forge.

Velvet salt of the kiln,

Dark pit of the heart,

Bulging gut of black coal.

The foundry is old.

The rust needs to be blasted.

Flame to purge the red scab,

Reveals the sinew of steel beneath.

Velvet black, chestnut soot, fire the forge.

A laugh heard under the click of ignition.

It fires the unexpected.

Flint the coal of cold steel and

It erupts, a spark, a miasma of stars in the dark.

What alchemy is at hand, the coal turns to diamond, but it brings no joy.

Put the diamond to your lips and

Kiss the searing cold.

Put the diamond in your mouth and

Roll your tongue as it slices flesh.

Put the diamond between your teeth

And wonder how the marble splinters, how the gums become ribboned.

Taste the iron that pours over that

Artificed gem, a cascade of scarlet that

Mars

What little beauty that dark forge could muster.

Do this, as this is all you know,

All you will ever know
